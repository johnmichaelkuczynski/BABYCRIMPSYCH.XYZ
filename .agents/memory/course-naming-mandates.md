# Course naming & marketing-copy mandates

These are explicit, non-negotiable user mandates. They override any "friendly/intro" framing inherited from a template or prior conversion.

## Audience
- The course is for **adults** — typically people starting a NEW discipline, often graduate students or established academics/professors. It is **not** for children.
- It is a **"basic"** course (entry-level), but "basic" ≠ "for kids".

## App / course name
- The app must get the **most literal, utilitarian name possible** — e.g. "Data Analytics", "Ethics", "Formal Logic", "Basic AI", "Basic Criminal Psychology". Never goofy/cute/funny.
- Rename rule: "X for Children" → "**Basic X**"; "Baby X" → "**Basic X**".
- Use that exact name everywhere it is referenced: app UI, README/replit.md, and inside the videos.

## Forbidden words
- The words **"children"**, **"baby"**, **"kid"** must appear nowhere in the app UI, video, README, or YouTube description — **UNLESS** the actual subject matter genuinely references them (e.g. developmental/evolutionary psychology, or criminology risk-factor/longitudinal content about children). Subject-matter uses are fine and should be kept.
- Never lead with belittling framing like **"No math!"**, **"No coding!"**, **"kid-friendly"**, "for curious students and grown-ups alike". It slanders substantial work. Drop it entirely; lead with the substance/wealth of content.
- Never mention the prior **California "critical thinking"/"docility" course** anywhere user-facing.

## YouTube description
- The title line MUST be exactly: `X — AI-Powered Course` (e.g. "Basic Criminal Psychology — AI-Powered Course").
- **Do not add descriptors** to that title (no "— an AI-Powered Course that teaches & grades!"). Just `X — AI-Powered Course`.
- Body should emphasize the **depth and amount of real content** (full unit, all topics, lessons, tutor, practice, graded coursework, integrity detection, diagnostics), professionally — not gimmicks.

**Why:** The user worked hard on substantial coursework and was angered by goofy, "for children", one-size-fits-all promo copy that could describe any of their courses. Treat it as a real, adult-level course.

**How to apply:** On any new course conversion or copy/video edit, apply the rename rule, purge forbidden words (except genuine subject matter), and use the fixed YouTube title format. Remember server-served course/unit/assignment titles and instructions are seeded content (reseed to update them), and that the same banned framing also hides in video-artifact social-preview meta — not just the visible UI.
